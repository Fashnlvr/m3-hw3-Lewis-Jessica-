About Claude Monet

Claude Monet (1840-1926) was a French painter and a leading figure in the Impressionist movement, which sought to capture the effects of light and atmosphere in painting. Monet's work is renowned for its innovative use of color and brushwork, and he is often associated with his series of paintings depicting water lilies, haystacks, and the Rouen Cathedral.

Monet's dedication to capturing natural scenes and his pioneering techniques have cemented his place as one of the most influential artists in art history. His approach to painting was revolutionary, focusing on capturing a moment in time with a vibrant and spontaneous style.

Notable Works

Monet's notable works include:

  • Water Lilies - A series of approximately 250 paintings depicting Monet's flower garden at Giverny.
  • Impression, Sunrise - The painting from which the Impressionist movement got its name.
  • Haystacks - A series of paintings showcasing the changing light and seasons on haystacks.
